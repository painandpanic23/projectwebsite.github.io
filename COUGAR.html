COUGAR / MOUNTAIN LION

(Puma concolor)

Here is a description of its key physical characteristics:

Size and Build:
Cougars have a long, slender body with a small, broad, and round head. Adult males are significantly larger than females, with males typically weighing between 115 and 220 pounds and females between 64 and 141 pounds. Including their long tails, adult cougars can measure from 6 to 8 feet in length.

Coloration:
The coat is typically a solid tawny or brownish-red color, which is why its scientific name concolor means "of one color." The fur is usually lighter on the underside, including the chin and belly. A distinguishing feature is the black or dark brown fur on the back of their ears and the tip of their tail.

Tail:
The tail is particularly long and heavy, often measuring a third of the animal's total body length. It serves as a counterbalance, helping the cougar maintain agility and balance when leaping and running.

Paws and Claws:
Cougars have large paws with five retractable claws on their forepaws and four on their hind paws. These large front paws and claws are an adaptation for grasping and holding prey. Their retractable claws are not visible in their tracks unless they are running on slippery terrain.

Vocalization:
Unlike "big cats" such as lions and tigers, cougars cannot roar. Instead, they produce a range of other sounds, including hisses, growls, purrs, and screams.

Physical Abilities:
Cougars are known for their incredible agility, strength, and leaping ability. They can leap horizontally over 20 feet and vertically as much as 18 feet, making them excellent climbers and ambush predators.
