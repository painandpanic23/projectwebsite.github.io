LION

(Panthera leo)

Here is a description of its key physical characteristics:

Size and Build:
Lions have a muscular, compact body with powerful forelegs, which they use to bring down prey. They are the second-largest big cat, after the tiger. Adult males are significantly larger than females, with males weighing between 330 and 550 pounds, while females weigh between 265 and 395 pounds.

Coat:
The lion's coat is short and varies in color from tawny yellow and orange-brown to silvery gray or even dark brown. Lion cubs are born with light spotting on their coats, which typically disappears as they mature.

Mane (Males):
The most distinguishing feature of a male lion is its thick, shaggy mane of hair that frames its face and covers its neck and shoulders. The color and length of the mane can vary greatly between individuals, ranging from blond to black. The mane serves to make the male look larger and more intimidating to rivals and to protect its neck during fights. Females do not have a mane.

Tail:
A lion's tail is long and has a distinctive tuft of dark fur at the tip.

Vocalization:
Lions are famous for their powerful roar, which can be heard from up to 5 miles away. They use roaring to communicate with other lions and to mark their territory. Unlike most other cats, lions are known to roar together as a pride.
