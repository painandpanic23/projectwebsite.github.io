LION

(Panthera leo)

The lion (Panthera leo) is a large, powerful cat native to Africa and, in a small, isolated population, India. It is the only cat species that is truly social, living in family units called prides.

TIGER

(Panthera tigris)

The tiger (Panthera tigris) is the largest of all the big cats and one of the most charismatic predators in the world. Known for its incredible strength, stealth, and distinctive striped coat, it is an apex predator native to a variety of habitats in Asia.

JAGUAR

(Panthera onca)

The jaguar (Panthera onca) is a powerful and impressive feline, and the largest cat in the Americas. It is a solitary predator, primarily found in the rainforests and wetlands of Central and South America.

LEOPARD

(Panthera pardus)

The leopard (Panthera pardus) is one of the most widespread and adaptable members of the big cat family. Known for its incredible stealth, agility, and opportunistic hunting behavior, it is found across a wide range of habitats in Africa and Asia.

SNOW LEOPARD

(Panthera uncia)

The snow leopard (Panthera uncia) is a large, reclusive cat native to the high-altitude mountains of Central and South Asia. Known as the "ghost of the mountains," its remarkable camouflage and shy nature make it one of the most elusive felines in the world.

CHEETAH

(Acinonyx jubatus)

The cheetah (Acinonyx jubatus) is a unique and highly specialized cat, renowned as the fastest land animal on Earth. Its entire physical structure is an extraordinary adaptation for speed.

COUGAR / MOUNTAIN LION

(Puma concolor)

The cougar (Puma concolor), also known by many other names such as mountain lion, puma, and panther, is a large, adaptable feline native to the Americas. It is the second-largest cat in the Western Hemisphere, after the jaguar.
